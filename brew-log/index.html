Two-Hearted Ale

Batch #2

We did our first batch of all grain. We attempted Bell’s Two-Hearted clone, and it went mostly without incident. Unfortunately the Bell's yeast did not arrive in time so I used Fermentis US-05, as recomended by Bell's. I miscalculated the strike temperature due the temperature in my garage. The mash sat at 140 degrees for 15 minutes before we could bring it up to 150. We will see how that affects the final product. We ended up with a batch volume of 5.5 gallons and an original gravity of 1.063. Not too shabby. Five days until we dry hop, so fingers crossed.

We both had so much fun. I cannot wait for the next batch. I will invite my brother Ben to do “Ruse Phantom Shore Citra Extra Pale”. I found this recipe on beerandbrewing.com. Ben wanted a lighter beer, and this extra pale ale fits the bill without being too complicated.

All Grain Brewing

I have Made the Investment into an All-Grain Brewing System

I’ve made the investment to all grain and will brew my first all grain in January. I have purchased a 15-gallon kettle with a welded ball cock valve and welded thermometer. Also, I have a 10-gallon modified cooler for the mash tun and a 5-gallon hot liquor tank. With a few more minor items I will be ready to go.

I’ve chosen Bell’s Two Hearted Ale clone to start. That was my choice in order to entice my brother, Paul, to assist, as Two Hearted is his absolute favorite. The version I am using can be found on Bell’s site here (Two Hearted Clone). I am intending to order Bell’s House Yeast. I have even gone so far to calculate the adjustments for the Kalamazoo water profile. I hope to recreate the true flavor of Bell’s flagship IPA.

Underdark Porter

Batch #1. An Aberrant Porter

The depths of Underdark are home to the most deviant aberrations. Mind Flayers, Aboleths, and Beholders rule the darkness. There an adventurer may find glory if he first does not lose his mind.

This is the first beer I wanted to try. Porters are a favorite of mine because of the malty complexity. This batch was an extract brew made with my brother Paul.

The batch turned out a lot lighter than you would expect from a porter. It was a very dark amber. The complexity did not meet my expectations and it clearly failed to have the characteristics of a Porter, so "ist das Leben". As much as it wasn’t a porter it was still a damn tasty beer.

  • 5 Gallons
  • O.G. 1.049
  • F.G. 1.013
  • IBU 25
  • %/Vol 5.1
  • BU/GU 0.49
  • Color 23 SRM
